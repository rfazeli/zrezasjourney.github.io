GoEmotions: A Dataset of Fine-Grained Emotions

Published: Sat 25 July 2020
By Reza Fazeli

In Research.

[THIS IS NOT COMPLETE YET!]

Contributions:

  1. Preparing a high-quality dataset of annotated 58 thousand reddit comments, labeled for 28 emotion categories (27 emotions and neutral). The previous largest language emotion dataset contains 40 thousand tweets divided into 13 emotion categories with suboptimal quality.
  2. They show a reasonable dissociation between their defined emotion categories making this dataset suitable for emotion classification
  3. Building a classification model on this dataset with impressive performance even on other datasets with different emotion taxanomies

Central Themes:

  1. Allow machines to understand human emotions using based on human language

Issues Addressed:

  1. Current datasets are small and have a limited emotion taxonomy

Possible Applications:

  1. This dataset can improve all the tweet sentiment analysis projects out there. One idea could be to use their trained model to tag tweet sentiments (and somehow verify the tagging quality) and creating a tweet emotion dataset

I haven't explore their dataset model yet and I plan to do this in another blog post.

While researching on this topic, I realized that there is an actual conference on social media NLP and there was an EmotionX Challenge as part of this conference last year on emotion detection.

I aim to describe their final dataset and their fine-tuned BERT model and I will skip the details of how they prepared the dataset. There is however a detailed description of how they gathered, annotated, and cleaned the dataset in the paper.
